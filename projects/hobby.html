Here I will show as to how a new self watering planter can be made with a used regular plastic milk can.

Self Watering Planter

Materials required

  • Empty plastic milk can. The taller the better!
  • A pair of Scissors
  • A knife or a long nail
  • Small river rocks or pebble rocks
  • Sharpie markers
  • Acrylic paints and brush to paint the planter

Instructions :

  1. Take the plastic milk can. Mark it with a sharpie just below the handle as shown in the picture 1.1.
  2. Using scissors, cut along the lines marked as shown in picture 1.2.
  3. Punch holes in the top half of the can with scissors or a sharp knife as shown in picture 1.3.
  4. Put some small pebble rocks inside the bottom half of the can as shown in the picture 1.4.
  5. Now invert the top half of the can and carefully place it inside of the bottom half as shown in picture 1.5.
  6. Paint the exterior of the can with your favorite acrylic paint to give it a fresh look.
  7. Viola, now you have a new planter ready!

In todays world, there are many items whether consumable or not, are packaged in plastic containers and glass bottles. As a result, a lot of recyclable plastic and glass ends up in the landfill if not reused or repurposed. According to some studies, it takes a plastic and glass bottles anywhere from 10 to 100 years to decompose [Credit: thisplastics.com]. Plastic buried deep in landfills can leach harmful chemicals that spread into the groundwater hence harming our environment and nature. With the recent pandemic, even more of plastics and glass has made it to the landfills. As a responsible human, it becomes our responsibility to try and reduce this waste as much as possible by renewing, reusing and repurposing these materials. To see how these recycled plastics are being used, check out recycleandrecoverplastics.org
